### Part 2: UNDERWATER DOLPHIN KICKS (20 min) ‚≠ê MOST IMPORTANT!

**Why this is critical**: Underwater kicks will save you 0.20 seconds - that's MORE THAN HALF of what you need!

**What "underwater dolphin kicks" means**:
- Both legs move together up and down (like a mermaid or dolphin)
- **"Streamline"** = arms straight above head, hands stacked, squeezing ears, body straight like an arrow
- Stay underwater as long as possible, kicking powerfully
- Goal: Reach 15 feet (the flags) before surfacing!

---

**SET 1: With fins** (if you have them) - 10 minutes

- [ ] **8 x 15 yards underwater kick on :45**

**How to do it (for Vihaan)**:
1. Start at the wall
2. Push off HARD in perfect streamline position
3. Do dolphin kicks - kick from your HIPS, not just knees
4. Count in your head: 1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8
5. Try to reach the flags (15 feet) before you breathe
6. When you MUST breathe, come up
7. Swim back to the start (or walk back along the side)
8. Rest until 45 seconds from when you started
9. Repeat 8 times

**Parent's job**:
- Stand at the 15-foot mark (at the flags) where Vihaan can see you
- Count his kicks OUT LOUD as he's underwater: "ONE! TWO! THREE! FOUR! FIVE! SIX! SEVEN! EIGHT!"
- When he surfaces, tell him:
  - "Great! You did 8 kicks and made it past the flags!" OR
  - "Good! You did 6 kicks and got to 12 feet - try for one more kick next time!"
- Use your phone's stopwatch - he should start the next one every 45 seconds
- **Encouragement is key!** "You're getting stronger!" "That was your best one yet!"

**What GOOD technique looks like**:
- ‚úÖ Arms perfectly straight, squeezed tight to ears
- ‚úÖ Body in straight line (no bend at waist)
- ‚úÖ Kicks from hips (whole legs moving)
- ‚úÖ Powerful kicks (you should see water moving!)
- ‚úÖ Stays 1-2 feet underwater (not at surface)

**Common mistakes**:
- ‚ùå Arms not squeezed to ears (creates drag)
- ‚ùå Head lifted (should be down between arms)
- ‚ùå Kicking only from knees (weak kicks)
- ‚ùå Coming up too early (before 10 feet)

### Part 3: STARTS FROM BLOCKS (15 min) ‚≠ê CRITICAL!

**Why this is critical**: A good start can save you 0.15-0.20 seconds!

- [ ] **10-12 butterfly starts**

**How to do it (for Vihaan)**:
1. Stand on starting blocks (or pool edge if no blocks)
2. Toes at edge, feet shoulder-width apart
3. Bend down, grab front edge with hands
4. When ready, say "GO!"
5. **PUSH HARD with legs** - explode off the blocks!
6. Throw arms forward, point them like an arrow
7. **Enter water at angle** - dive DOWN into water, not flat
8. Immediately get into streamline (hands stacked, arms squeezing ears)
9. Do **5-6 dolphin kicks** underwater
10. When you reach 15 feet (flags), start swimming butterfly
11. Swim to the wall (or just take 2-3 strokes and stop)
12. Get out, walk back, rest 45-60 seconds
13. Repeat 10-12 times

**Parent's job (VERY IMPORTANT)**:
- **Safety first**: Make sure pool is at least 6 feet deep for diving. If not, practice from pool edge pushing off.
- Stand where you can see him enter the water
- **Time each start**:
  - Start timer when his feet leave the blocks
  - Stop timer when his head breaks the surface to breathe
  - **Goal time**: 4-5 seconds from start to surface
- Count his kicks OUT LOUD: "1-2-3-4-5-6!"
- Watch where he surfaces - should be at or past the flags (15 feet)

**What GOOD start looks like**:
- ‚úÖ Powerful leg push (you hear/see the explosion off blocks)
- ‚úÖ Enters water hands-first at 45-degree angle (like arrow)
- ‚úÖ Small splash (not belly flop)
- ‚úÖ Immediately streamlines underwater
- ‚úÖ 5-6 powerful kicks visible underwater
- ‚úÖ Surfaces at 15 feet (the flags)
- ‚úÖ Takes 4-5 seconds from start to surface

**Common mistakes**:
- ‚ùå Weak push off blocks (not using full leg power)
- ‚ùå Enters flat (belly flop - big splash)
- ‚ùå Arms not straight when entering
- ‚ùå Only does 2-3 kicks (needs 5-6!)
- ‚ùå Comes up too early (before 12 feet)

**What to say**:
- If good: "Perfect! 5 kicks, reached the flags, 4.3 seconds - that's race-ready!"
- If needs work: "Good push! Next time try to get one more kick before surfacing"
- Always end positive: "You're getting better every time!"

### Part 4: FAST BUTTERFLY TURNS (10 min)

**Why this matters**: A fast turn can save 0.08 seconds!

- [ ] **8 x 50 butterfly - focus on the turn**

**How to do it (for Vihaan)**:
1. Swim easy butterfly to the wall (first 25)
2. **IMPORTANT - Last 2 strokes before wall: NO BREATHING!**
3. Touch wall with BOTH hands at the SAME TIME (this is a rule!)
4. Quick tuck - pull knees to chest FAST
5. Plant feet on wall
6. Push off HARD on your side or back (not on belly!)
7. Do 3-4 dolphin kicks underwater
8. Surface at about 10 feet from the wall
9. Swim easy butterfly to the other end (second 25)
10. Rest 20-30 seconds
11. Repeat 8 times

**Parent's job**:
- Stand at the wall where you can see the turn
- Watch for BOTH hands touching at the same time (if only one hand touches, it's illegal in a race!)
- Count how fast the turn is: from touch to push-off should be about 1 second
- Count the kicks after the turn: should see 3-4 kicks
- Watch where he surfaces: should be about 10 feet from the wall

**What GOOD turn looks like**:
- ‚úÖ No breath on last 2 strokes (head stays down)
- ‚úÖ Both hands touch wall at same time
- ‚úÖ Quick tuck (knees to chest in less than 1 second)
- ‚úÖ Explosive push off wall
- ‚úÖ 3-4 powerful dolphin kicks
- ‚úÖ Surfaces at 10 feet

**Common mistakes**:
- ‚ùå Breathes right before wall (slows down)
- ‚ùå Only one hand touches wall (illegal!)
- ‚ùå Slow tuck (wastes time)
- ‚ùå Weak push off wall
- ‚ùå Only 1-2 kicks (needs 3-4!)

**What to say**:

### THE RACE - 50 BUTTERFLY

**Remember the 3 Secrets to Success**:

**Secret #1**: 6 kicks off the start ‚Üí reach the flags!
**Secret #2**: Don't breathe last 2 strokes into walls
**Secret #3**: SPRINT the last 25 yards!

**The Race Plan** (what Vihaan should think about):

**Before getting on the blocks**:
- Take off your sweats at the last minute
- Adjust your goggles (make sure they're tight!)
- Shake out your arms
- One more deep breath
- Think: "I've practiced this. I'm ready. I can do this!"

**On the blocks**:
- Step up when they call your heat
- Toes at the edge
- Grab the front of the block
- Listen for "Take your mark..."
- Get ready position (bent down, ready to explode)
- Wait for the beep... **GO!!!**

**THE START** (0-15 feet):
1. **EXPLODE off the blocks!** (push HARD with your legs!)
2. Enter water deep (hands first, like an arrow going down)
3. **Tight streamline** (arms locked, squeeze ears)
4. **Count your kicks**: 1-2-3-4-5-6 (dolphin kicks!)
5. Reach the flags (15 feet) before surfacing
6. This alone saves you 0.20 seconds!

**FIRST 25** (swimming to the turn):
1. Start swimming butterfly (strong, smooth strokes)
2. High elbows when your arms come out of the water
3. Quick recovery (hands move fast over the water)
4. Breathe every 2 or 3 strokes (find your rhythm)
5. **IMPORTANT**: Last 2 strokes before the wall - NO BREATHING!
6. Keep your head down and swim fast into the wall

**THE TURN** (at 25 yards):
1. Touch wall with **BOTH hands** at the same time (very important!)
2. Quick tuck (pull knees to chest FAST!)
3. Plant feet on wall
4. Push off HARD (as hard as you can!)
5. **3-4 dolphin kicks** off the turn
6. Surface at about 10 feet from the wall
7. This saves you 0.08 seconds!

**LAST 25** (the sprint!):
1. This is where you WIN!
2. **SPRINT** - swim as fast as you can!
3. High elbows, quick hands
4. Fast arms - don't slow down!
5. Last 10 yards - FASTEST swimming of the whole race!
6. **IMPORTANT**: Last 2 strokes - NO BREATHING!
7. Keep your head down
8. **LUNGE** to the wall (reach out as far as you can!)
9. **TOUCH!!!**

**After you touch**:
1. Look at the clock - what's your time?
2. Breathe! (You probably held your breath a lot!)
3. Remember your time
4. Get out of the pool when the official tells you
5. Check the scoreboard for your official time
